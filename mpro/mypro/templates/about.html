    ABOUT US

    Your health is our priority!

    Personalized treatment plans and individual outcomes will be enhanced by the capacity to forecast a cancer patient's response to radiotherapy and risk of experiencing negative long-term health impacts. As exposure to radiation can cause dysfunctional telomere pathologies that coincidentally overlap with many radiation-induced late effects, ranging from degenerative conditions like fibrosis and cardiovascular disease to proliferative pathologies like cancer, telomeres represent a compelling biomarker of individual radiosensitivity and risk. Here, telomere length will be measured using Quantitative fluorescence in situ hybridization (Q-FISH) in a group of cancer patients receiving intensity-modulated radiation therapy (IMRT). Chromosome abnormalities will be evaluated in order to determine genome instability and improve customized treatment.
